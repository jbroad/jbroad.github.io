Social scientists often face serious data limitations that impede research into important questions. Frustrated with these limitations, I learned how to scrape web data and metadata with Python. I have more data than I know what to do with. One dataset I have compiled using webscraping consists of New York Times articles shared on the NYTimes Twitter handle from 2012 through 2018. The dataset includes the full text of each tweet, its engagement metrics, as well as each article’s headline, body, and keywords. The articles were then categorized into topics using content analysis (LDA) and coded manually.
